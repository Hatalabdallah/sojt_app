Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 22, 2025

I. Introduction

At Infronte, we value your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, and safeguard your data when you visit our website, sojt.infronte.co.uk (the “Service”). By using our Service, you consent to the data practices described in this policy.

II. Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide and improve our Service. This includes:

A. Personal Data

While using our Service, we may ask you to provide us with certain personally identifiable information, which may include:

  • Contact Information: This includes your email address, first name, last name, phone number, and physical address, which we use to communicate with you.
  • Demographic Information: We may collect information such as your date of birth, educational background, and self-reported income and employment details to better understand our user base.
  • Account Information: When you create an account, we collect your username, password, and any other information you provide during the registration process.

B. Usage Data

We automatically collect information that your browser sends whenever you visit our Service. This may include:

  • IP Address: Your computer’s Internet Protocol address, which helps us identify your location.
  • Browser Information: Details about your browser type and version, which assist us in optimizing our website for different platforms.
  • Page Visits: The specific pages of our Service that you visit, along with the time and date of your visit and the time spent on those pages.
  • Device Information: Unique device identifiers and other diagnostic data that help us understand how our Service is accessed and used.

C. Location Data

With your permission, we may use and store information about your location to enhance your experience with our Service. You can enable or disable location services through your device settings at any time.

D. Tracking & Cookies Data

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to monitor activity on our Service and store certain information. Cookies are small files that may include an anonymous unique identifier. You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. However, if you do not accept cookies, you may not be able to use some portions of our Service.

III. How We Use Your Data

Infronte uses the collected data for various purposes, including:

  • Service Provision: To provide and maintain our Service, ensuring its functionality and security.
  • Updates and Notifications: To notify you about changes to our Service, including new features or updates.
  • Interactive Features: To allow you to participate in interactive features when you choose to do so, enhancing your user experience.
  • Customer Support: To respond to your inquiries and provide assistance, ensuring you have the help you need.
  • Data Analysis: To gather analysis or valuable information that helps us improve our Service based on user feedback and usage patterns.
  • Usage Monitoring: To monitor how our Service is used, which helps us enhance user experience and address any issues.
  • Technical Issue Resolution: To detect, prevent, and address technical issues that may arise, ensuring the integrity of our Service.
  • Marketing Communications: To send you newsletters, marketing, or promotional materials unless you have opted out of receiving such communications.

IV. Legal Basis for Processing Personal Data

If you are from the European Economic Area (EEA), Infronte’s legal basis for collecting and using your personal information depends on the information we collect and the context in which we collect it. We may process your Personal Data because:

  • We need to perform a contract with you, such as providing our services.
  • You have given us permission to do so, such as when you sign up for our newsletter.
  • The processing is in our legitimate interests and it is not overridden by your rights, such as improving our services.
  • To comply with the law, such as fulfilling legal obligations.

V. Data Retention

We will retain your Personal Data only for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide you with our Service. We will retain and use your Personal Data to the extent necessary to comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our legal agreements and policies.

VI. Data Transfer

Your information, including Personal Data, may be transferred to — and maintained on — computers located outside of your state, province, country, or other governmental jurisdiction where the data protection laws may differ from those of your jurisdiction. Your consent to this Privacy Policy followed by your submission of such information represents your agreement to that transfer.

VII. Disclosure of Data

We may disclose Personal Data for a business or commercial purpose, including:

  • Service Providers: To contractors, service providers, and other third parties we use to support our business operations.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws and regulations, ensuring we meet our legal obligations.
  • Protection of Rights: To protect the rights, property, or safety of Infronte, our users, or others, including in the event of legal disputes.

VIII. Security of Data

The security of your data is important to us. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your Personal Data, please remember that no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee its absolute security, but we take reasonable steps to protect your information.

IX. Your Data Protection Rights

If you are a resident of the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain data protection rights. Infronte aims to take reasonable steps to allow you to correct, amend, delete, or limit the use of your Personal Data. These rights include:

  • Access: The right to access, update, or delete the information we have on you.
  • Rectification: The right to rectify any inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Objection: The right to object to our processing of your Personal Data.
  • Restriction: The right to request the restriction of processing your Personal Data.
  • Data Portability: The right to request the transfer of your Personal Data to another organization.
  • Withdrawal of Consent: The right to withdraw your consent at any time where Infronte relied on your consent to process your personal information.

X. Children’s Privacy

Our Service is not intended for anyone under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from anyone under the age of 18. If you are a parent or guardian and you are aware that your child has provided us with Personal Data, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

XI. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes to stay informed about how we are protecting your information.

XII. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please do not hesitate to contact us at: privacy@infronte.co.uk. We are here to assist you with any inquiries or concerns you may have regarding your privacy and data protection.
